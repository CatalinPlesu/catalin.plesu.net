Antiresume

The other side of the projects page. What didn't work, what was abandoned, and where I made obviously wrong decisions.

Moldova Job Market Scraper

The scraper worked fine. It ran on a schedule, logged data, built up a decent dataset of Moldovan job postings over time. Then I "shipped" a frontend dashboard. The dashboard loaded the entire SQLite file — over 100 MB — into the browser to query it with SQL.js. I knew this was wrong but assumed it would be fine anyway. It wasn't — and that's before considering anyone on a phone or a modest laptop. Currently being rebuilt with a real backend. The data is still there.

Oroboros

Was supposed to be a SaaS. I built a landing page, then stopped and thought about it. I don't use any paid SaaS product myself, and building one didn't align with what I actually value. DHH did something worth thinking about with Once — sell software once, no subscriptions, no lock-in. I should do something closer to that if I build a product. The landing page is gone.

Eden

A 3D game in Rust using Bevy. Got working basic movement, some physics, random terrain generation, and flying whales that traveled on Bézier curves and spawned oranges for reasons I no longer fully remember. The player could collect oranges for a score. It was too hard for me to continue at the time. Removed from public listing.

8-bit CPU

The ALU, register file, control unit, and memory bus all work. The oscillator is still a black box in the Logisim schematic. I got to the point where the oscillator was the missing piece and stopped. The CPU does not run. I built an assembler for an ISA the CPU can't execute yet.

ErgoType

The algorithm works. The results are measurable — ~10–15% improvement over Asset on the tested corpus. I'm writing this on Asset.

TimeSheet Bot

Runs in production. I invited myself. Zero other users. Technically a success at what it does; practically a tool I built and use alone.

Canti IoT

Built as a bachelor's thesis. The hardware works, the app works, Bluetooth connects. The device lives in a drawer. I don't carry it anymore.

CV Builder

Live and functional. I'm not sure anyone other than me has used it to generate a resume.

The common thread: I build things to understand them, not to grow them. Most of these did their job — I learned what I needed to learn. The ones that didn't even do that are the honest failures.
