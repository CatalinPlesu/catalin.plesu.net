A note for employers and recruiters.

I'm 25. I've been writing software professionally for three years — a year in automotive embedded firmware under ISO 26262, then an internship and full-time role in backend .NET at Jivy Group. Before that, a bachelor's in IT at Technical University of Moldova and enough side projects to have touched Rust, Ruby, Python, Elixir, Dart, and assembly. The breadth is genuine. The current depth is C# and systems programming.

I'm junior, and I mean that precisely: I design and own the modules and services I'm responsible for. I don't drive company-level architecture — I'm not in a position to yet, and I know it. What I do bring is the habit of understanding systems before touching them, a low tolerance for guessing when I could read the source, and the discipline that comes from a year in a safety-critical codebase where being wrong has physical consequences.

I'm currently doing my MSc thesis on keyboard layout optimization — a genetic algorithm that searches for ergonomically better layouts. Not because keyboards are my career, but because I built a custom keyboard, wondered whether the layout I was practicing was actually justified, and decided to find out properly. This is roughly how I approach most things I get curious about.

I run a homelab because I'd rather understand the stack than outsource it. I use NixOS because reproducibility matters to me. I keep notes because I want to actually think about things, not just collect them.

If you're looking for someone who already has ten years of experience, I'm not that. If you're looking for someone who will read the code, ask the uncomfortable questions, and own their work — I'm available.
