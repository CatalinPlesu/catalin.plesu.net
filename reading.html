Reading

Books that stuck. Not a complete list.

Fiction

Dune — Frank Herbert. Ecology, power, and the trap of the hero who arrives to save you.

The End of Eternity — Isaac Asimov. Time travel as a bureaucracy problem. Also a warning about optimization at the wrong scale.

Non-fiction

Walden — Henry David Thoreau. What do you actually need? Harder to answer than it looks.

In progress

The Lessons of History — Will & Ariel Durant.

Queue

Summa Technologiae — Stanisław Lem. Philosophy of science and cybernetics from 1964.

The World-Ending Fire — Wendell Berry. Essays on land, technology, and what gets lost.
